Dialogue Writing Guide

Instructions for Writing Effective Dialogues:

  1. Make it natural -- Write how people actually speak, with contractions and interruptions.
  2. Show character through speech -- Use vocabulary and sentence structure that reflects personality.
  3. Use proper formatting -- New speaker = new paragraph, no quotation marks.
  4. Include action beats -- Add small actions to show what characters are doing while speaking like: hmm, huh, oh,....
  5. Advance the plot -- Every dialogue should move the story forward or reveal character.
  6. Keep it concise -- Avoid long monologues unless necessary for character.
  7. Vary sentence length -- Mix short and long sentences for rhythm.
  8. Read aloud -- Test if it sounds natural by speaking it.
  9. Use subtext -- Sometimes what's not said is as important as what is said.
  10. Limit exposition -- Avoid characters explaining things they would already know.

Friends Making Weekend Plans

Riya   : Hey, are you free this weekend? I was thinking we could do something fun.

Anuj  : Actually yeah! I'm done with my exams finally. What did you have in mind?

Riya   : Well, there's that new adventure park that opened near Nagarjun. We could go hiking and try the zip-lining.

Anuj  : Sounds awesome! Though... I'm kind of broke after buying textbooks. How much is it?

Riya   : It's 800 rupees, but I can lend you the money if you want. You can pay me back whenever.

Anuj  : You're the best! Let's go Saturday morning then? I'll bring snacks for the hike.

Parent-Teen Conflict About Grades

Mother   : Suman, your math teacher called today. She said you haven't submitted the last three assignments.

Nirmala  : I know, I know. I'll do them this weekend.

Mother   : This weekend? Your exams start Monday! Why do you keep procrastinating?

Nirmala  : It's not that simple! The new topics are really hard and when I don't understand, I just... freeze.

Mother   : Why didn't you tell me? I could have arranged a tutor weeks ago.

Nirmala  : I didn't want to disappoint you. You always expect me to handle everything perfectly.

Job Interview at a Tech Company

Manager : So, Priya, your portfolio shows great design work. Tell me about a challenging project you handled.

Priya     : Thank you. The most challenging was redesigning our college website. The main issue was creating a system that worked for both students and professors.

Manager : Interesting. How did you approach the different user needs?

Priya     : I conducted surveys with both groups, then created separate dashboards with a unified login. Professors needed grade submission tools while students wanted assignment tracking.

Manager : What would you change if you did it again?

Priya     : I'd involve the IT department earlier. We had to redo some backend work because I didn't understand their constraints initially.

Customer Complaining About a Product

Customer : Excuse me, I bought this blender here last week and it stopped working already.

Manager  : I'm sorry to hear that. Do you have the receipt with you?

Customer : Yes, here. I only used it twice to make smoothies. Then it started smoking!

Manager  : Hmm, did you by chance put ice cubes in it? This model isn't designed for crushing ice.

Customer : Well, the ad showed it making frozen drinks! How else would you make a smoothie?

Manager  : You're absolutely right. Let me replace it with our professional model that can handle ice. I'll throw in an extended warranty too.
