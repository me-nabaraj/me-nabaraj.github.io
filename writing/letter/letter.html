Letter Writing Guide

Formal Letter Instructions

  1. Sender's Address -- Place your address at the top right
  2. Date -- Write the date below your address
  3. Recipient's Address -- Left-align below the date
  4. Salutation -- Use "Dear [Title] [Last Name]:"
  5. Subject Line -- Briefly state the purpose
  6. Body -- Divide into clear paragraphs
  7. Closing -- Use "Yours sincerely" or "Yours faithfully"
  8. Signature -- Leave space for handwritten signature
  9. Printed Name -- Include below signature
  10. Enclosures -- Note if documents are attached

Informal Letter Instructions

  1. Date -- Optional, can be at the top right
  2. Salutation -- Use first name (e.g., "Dear Sarah,")
  3. Opening -- Start with friendly greeting
  4. Body -- Write conversationally in paragraphs
  5. Closing -- Use warm phrases ("Best wishes," "Love,")
  6. Signature -- Just your first name is fine
  7. Tone -- Keep it personal and friendly
  8. Content -- Share news, ask questions
  9. Length -- Can be short or long as needed
  10. Format -- More flexible than formal letters

Dear Anjali,

I hope this letter finds you well. It's been too long since we last met, and I wanted to catch up and share some exciting news.

I've recently moved to Pokhara for a new job opportunity, and I'm absolutely loving the city. The view of the mountains every morning is breathtaking! My new apartment is small but cozy, and it's just a short walk from Phewa Lake.

How have you been? How's your new teaching position working out? I'd love to hear all about your experiences. Let's plan to meet up during the Dashain holidays - maybe we could take that trip to Chitwan we always talked about?

Please write back when you get a chance. I've included some photos of my new place and the amazing mountain views.

Looking forward to hearing from you,

Ramesh
