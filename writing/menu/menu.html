Menu Writing Lesson

Instructions

  1. Start with a Title: Begin by writing a creative and appealing title for your menu, such as “Café Delight Menu” or “Tasty Treats,” to grab attention and set the tone. You can also write the name and address of the hotel. Adding a tagline is impressive!
  2. Organize into Sections: Divide your menu into clear sections like “Starters,” “Main Course,” “Desserts,” and “Beverages” to make it easy to read and navigate.
  3. List Items with Descriptions: Write the name of each dish followed by a short, appetizing description. Keep it simple but tempting.
  4. Add Prices: Include a price for each item aligned neatly next to the dish name or description to help customers decide.
  5. Use Clear and Neat Formatting: Write in a simple, readable handwriting and style, and use bullet points, numbers, or spacing to separate items so the menu looks organized and professional.
  6. Check Spelling and Grammar: Proofread your menu to ensure there are no mistakes, as errors can make it look unprofessional.
