How to Write a Movie Review

Step-by-step guide with example for *Hostel Returns*

1. Introduction (50-60 words)

Introduce the movie and set the tone of your review. Mention the movie name, director, lead actors, genre, and the release year. A brief mention of the central theme (college life, friendship, and humor) is important.

Example: "Hostel Returns, directed by Suraj Subba Nalbo, is a popular Nepali comedy that revolves around the hilarious lives of college students living in a hostel. Starring Pradeep Khadka, Barsha Raut, and Buddhi Tamang, the movie explores themes of friendship, love, and the chaotic yet heartwarming experiences of hostel life."

Side Note: This part should briefly let the reader know what the movie is about, without giving too much away.

2. Plot Summary (60-80 words)

Provide a brief summary of the movie’s storyline. Focus on key events without revealing major spoilers. Emphasize the comedic and emotional moments of the protagonist.

Example: "The movie follows the life of a group of college students living in a hostel. They face challenges in their academic and personal lives while navigating the complexities of friendships and relationships. The film balances humor with moments of emotional depth, showing how these students deal with their ups and downs, all while being surrounded by quirky characters and hilarious situations."

Side Note: Keep it short, no spoilers, and don’t get into too much detail about the ending.

3. Characters and Performances (50-60 words)

Evaluate the performances of the main characters. Comment on how the lead actors portray their characters and how they contribute to the overall story.

Example: "Pradeep Khadka’s portrayal of the protagonist is both humorous and endearing, capturing the essence of a young college student trying to balance his life and studies. Barsha Raut’s performance as the love interest adds a lighthearted touch, while Buddhi Tamang provides comic relief, making the characters relatable and engaging throughout the movie."

Side Note: Focus on how the actors portrayed emotions and whether the characters felt realistic.

4. Direction and Cinematography (40-50 words)

Discuss the technical aspects of the movie such as direction and cinematography. Mention how visuals contributed to the movie's overall theme and humor.

Example: "Suraj Subba Nalbo’s direction brings a perfect balance of humor and drama, giving the audience a delightful experience. The cinematography captures the lively energy of hostel life, using bright colors and fun shots that complement the movie’s lighthearted tone. The use of wide-angle shots during group scenes adds to the chaotic yet joyful environment."

Side Note: Avoid being too technical. Focus on how the visuals helped tell the story.

5. Themes and Message (40-50 words)

Highlight the movie's core themes and what message it conveys to the audience.

Example: "Hostel Returns explores themes of friendship, loyalty, and youthful rebellion. The film conveys a message of enjoying life’s journey, even in the face of difficulties. It emphasizes the importance of standing by your friends and cherishing moments that shape one's youth."

Side Note: This is an important part. Focus on what the movie is trying to communicate to the audience.

6. Conclusion and Rating (30-40 words)

Wrap up your review with your overall opinion and give a final rating. Would you recommend the movie to others?

Example: "Overall, Hostel Returns is a fun and entertaining film that captures the spirit of youth, college life, and the bond of friendship. It’s a must-watch for those who enjoy comedy and lighthearted dramas. I would give it 4/5."

Side Note: Keep your tone positive or constructive, but be honest. Don’t be too harsh if you didn’t like the movie.
