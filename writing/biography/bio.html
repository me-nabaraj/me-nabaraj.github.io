Biography Writing Guide

Teaching students to write compelling biographies with structure, accuracy, and engagement

How to Write a Biography

A biography tells the true story of someone's life. Follow these steps to write an engaging and well-structured biography.

1

Choose Your Subject

Select a person with an interesting life story. Research their background, achievements, challenges, and impact on others.

2

Research Thoroughly

Gather information from reliable sources. Look for key dates, important events, accomplishments, and personal details.

3

Create a Timeline

Organize events in chronological order. Identify the beginning, middle, and end of their life story.

4

Focus on Key Events

Highlight the most important moments: birth, education, career milestones, challenges overcome, and legacy.

5

Show Personality

Include personal traits, quotes, and anecdotes that reveal the subject's character and values.

6

Write with Accuracy

Stick to facts and verify information. A good biography is truthful and respectful.

Sample Biographies (130 Words Each)

Study these examples to understand how to structure a concise yet complete biography.

Marie Curie

1867-1934 Scientist Nobel Prize Winner
Marie Curie was a pioneering physicist and chemist born in Warsaw, Poland. She moved to Paris to study at the Sorbonne, where she met her future husband, Pierre Curie. Together, they discovered two new elements: polonium and radium. Her groundbreaking research on radioactivity earned her two Nobel Prizes—one in Physics (1903) and one in Chemistry (1911)—making her the first person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences. During World War I, she developed mobile X-ray units to help treat wounded soldiers. Despite facing gender discrimination, Curie's dedication to science changed medicine forever. She died from exposure to radiation, a risk she knowingly took to advance human knowledge.

Nelson Mandela

1918-2013 Activist President
Nelson Mandela was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary who became his country's first Black president. Born in the village of Mvezo, he studied law and joined the African National Congress (ANC) to fight racial discrimination. For his activism against the apartheid government, he was imprisoned for 27 years, mostly on Robben Island. Despite harsh conditions, Mandela became a symbol of resistance and hope. After his release in 1990, he worked to end apartheid peacefully and won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993. Elected president in 1994, he focused on reconciliation between racial groups and established the Truth and Reconciliation Commission. Mandela's leadership demonstrated that forgiveness can unite a divided nation.

Malala Yousafzai

1997-Present Activist Nobel Laureate
Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani education activist and the youngest Nobel Prize laureate. Born in Mingora, Pakistan, she began advocating for girls' education at age 11, writing a blog for the BBC about life under Taliban rule. In 2012, a Taliban gunman shot her for speaking out, but she survived and continued her activism. After recovering in the UK, she founded the Malala Fund to support girls' education worldwide. At 17, she became the youngest recipient of the Nobel Peace Prize. Malala graduated from Oxford University in 2020. Her courage has inspired millions to value education as a fundamental right. Today, she continues to fight for every girl's right to 12 years of free, safe, quality education.

Teaching Tips for Smartboard Use

  • Start with the samples: Display each biography and ask students to identify key elements before revealing the analysis.
  • Use the interactive tool: Build a biography together as a class to demonstrate the process step-by-step.
  • Highlight structure: Show how each biography follows a similar pattern: introduction → early life → achievements → legacy.
  • Discuss word economy: Point out how 130 words can tell a complete life story when focused on the most important details.
  • Compare subjects: Have students compare how different lives are presented using the same biographical structure.
