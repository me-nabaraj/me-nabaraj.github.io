Writing Obligations and Prohibitions

Instructions for Writing Obligations and Prohibitions

  • Understand the Purpose: Identify the specific rules and guidelines needed for a particular place or situation.
  • Use Clear and Direct Language: Write in simple and precise sentences.
  • Structure the Statements Properly: Use words like "must," "should," "is required to" for obligations, and "must not," "is not allowed" for prohibitions.
  • Use Variety in Sentence Formation: Avoid repeating the same sentence structure.
  • Make It Specific and Relevant: Tailor the rules to the setting.
  • Maintain a Formal Tone: Use a respectful and authoritative tone.
