Recipe Writing Basics

Writing a recipe is more than just listing ingredients and steps. A well-written recipe communicates clearly, anticipates questions, and guides the cook to success.

Key Elements of a Recipe:

  • Title: Clear and descriptive name of the dish
  • Description: Brief introduction to the dish
  • Prep Time & Cook Time: Helps with meal planning
  • Servings/Yield: How many people it serves
  • Ingredients: Listed in order of use with precise measurements
  • Instructions: Step-by-step directions in logical order
  • Notes/Tips: Helpful suggestions for success

Pro Tip:

Always test your recipe before sharing it! Cook it exactly as written to ensure the instructions are clear and the measurements are accurate.

Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

Dessert • 24 cookies

Ingredients

  • 2¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, beat butter and both sugars until creamy.
  4. Add eggs one at a time, then vanilla, beating well after each addition.
  5. Gradually beat in flour mixture. Stir in chocolate chips.
  6. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto prepared sheets.
  7. Bake 9-11 minutes until golden brown. Cool on wire racks.

Vegetable Stir-Fry

Main Dish • 4 servings

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 bell peppers, sliced
  • 2 carrots, julienned
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large wok or skillet over high heat.
  2. Add onion and stir-fry for 2 minutes until slightly softened.
  3. Add peppers, carrots, broccoli, and snap peas. Stir-fry for 5-6 minutes.
  4. Add garlic and ginger, cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, honey, and rice vinegar.
  6. Pour sauce over vegetables and toss to coat. Cook for 1 more minute.
  7. Serve immediately over rice or noodles.

Fresh Garden Salad

Side Dish • 6 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 head romaine lettuce, chopped
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • ½ cup croutons
  • ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Wash and dry all vegetables thoroughly.
  2. In a large salad bowl, combine lettuce, cucumber, tomatoes, onion, and carrot.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, vinegar, mustard, salt, and pepper.
  4. Pour dressing over salad and toss gently to coat.
  5. Sprinkle with croutons and Parmesan cheese just before serving.
  6. Serve immediately for best texture.

Banana Smoothie

Beverage • 2 servings

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 cup milk (any type)
  • 2 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup ice cubes
  • Optional: ½ cup berries or 1 tbsp peanut butter

Instructions

  1. Peel bananas and break into chunks.
  2. Place all ingredients in a blender in the order listed.
  3. Blend on high speed until smooth and creamy, about 1 minute.
  4. If too thick, add a little more milk. If too thin, add more ice.
  5. Taste and adjust sweetness if needed.
  6. Pour into glasses and serve immediately.

Writing Tips

Use Active Voice

Write instructions in active voice: "Chop the onions" instead of "The onions should be chopped."

Be Specific

Instead of "add some salt," write "add 1 teaspoon of salt." Precision matters in recipes.

List Ingredients in Order

List ingredients in the order they're used. This helps cooks prepare efficiently.

Include Preparation Details

Specify how ingredients should be prepared: "1 onion, finely diced" or "2 cloves garlic, minced."
