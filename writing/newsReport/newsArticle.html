How to Write a News Report

Tip: A news article should be clear, concise, and factual. It typically includes a headline, byline, dateline, lead, body, and conclusion.

1. Headline

The headline should grab attention and summarize the main point of the article. It should be short and impactful.

"Use action words and keep it under 10 words for maximum impact!"
Example: "Local School Wins National Science Competition"

2. Byline

The byline credits the author of the article. It usually appears below the headline.

Example: "By Manoj Bista"

3. Dateline

The dateline indicates the location and date of the story. It typically appears at the beginning of the report.

Example: "Butwal, October 10"

4. Lead Paragraph

The lead paragraph should answer the 5 W's and 1 H: Who, What, When, Where, Why, and How. It should be concise and engaging.

Example: "On October 10, 2023, Green Valley High School won the National Science Competition held in New Delhi, showcasing innovative projects in renewable energy."

5. Body

The body provides additional details, quotes, and background information. Organize the information in order of importance (inverted pyramid style).

Example: "The winning team, comprising five students from Class 10, presented an innovative project on solar energy efficiency. 'We wanted to create something that could benefit rural areas,' said team leader Riya Sharma."

6. Conclusion

Summarize the key points and provide a closing thought or call to action.

Example: "The competition, organized by the Ministry of Education, aims to encourage young minds to explore scientific solutions to real-world problems. Green Valley High School's victory has set a new benchmark for excellence in science education."

Here is a sample news report for reference:

Local School Wins National Science Competition

By Manoj Bista

Butwal, October 10

Green Valley High School won the National Science Competition held in Kathmandu, showcasing innovative projects in renewable energy.

The winning team, comprising five students from Class 10, presented an innovative project on solar energy efficiency. 'We wanted to create something that could benefit rural areas,' said team leader Riya Sharma. Their project demonstrated how solar panels could be made more affordable and efficient using locally available materials.

The judges were particularly impressed by the team's use of recycled materials to reduce costs. 'Their approach is not only innovative but also sustainable,' remarked Dr. Anil Kumar, one of the competition judges. The project has already garnered interest from local NGOs looking to implement the solution in rural communities.

The competition, organized by the Ministry of Education, aims to encourage young minds to explore scientific solutions to real-world problems. Green Valley High School's victory has set a new benchmark for excellence in science education.

Bus Hits Car, 3 Dead

by Staff Reporter, The Nagarik News

Butwal, 23rd June

A bus travelling from Narayanghat to Pokhara rammed into a car yesterday, killing 3 people on the spot, near Kurintar during the early hours of the morning.

In an attempt to overtake the lorry in front of it, the bus lost control leading to a head-on collision with a car which was coming from the opposite direction. All the passengers in the car were killed on the spot. The passengers of the bus who had suffered minor injuries were given first aid at the nearby hospital. An eyewitness said that the bus driver was at fault as he was trying to overtake at a spot where there was no space.

"We suddenly heard a big bang followed by an outcry," said Manish Shrestha, one of the bus passangers. A patrolling police van, which was nearby, immediately arrived at the accident site and directed the rescue operations. The bus driver was arrested on the spot. An enquiry has been initiated.

It has been observed that this part of the road has been witnessing numerous accidents primarily due to vehicles travelling at speed due to lack of speed breakers, which are necessary in such a crowded area.

Residents Launch Campaign Against Child Labour

By Ankit Mourya

Butwal, 24th January

A campaign to raise awareness and enforce a ban on the heinous crime of child labour was launched last week by Buddha Colony Resident Welfare Association of Manigram in Tilottama Municipality.

The municipality has an extended market area where migrants from the northern hills and surrounding villages dwell for a living. They force their children even below 14 years of age to work in dhabas, in cycle repair shops, as well as in pavement stalls. The poor children remain illiterate just like their parents.

The campaign received the support of residents, businessmen and labour coming together and participating wholeheartedly.

“Working as a labour during the formative years hinders the all-round growth of the children,” said Mr. Yamlal Pandey, a veteran social worker, addressing the people in the concluding program. Participants held a peaceful march with posters and slogans in favour of banning child labour.

A headline is the title of the news article. It should be catchy and summarize the main point.

The byline credits the author of the news. It usually appears below the headline.

The dateline indicates the location and date of the story. It typically appears at the beginning of the article.

The lead paragraph is the most important part of the news report. It answers the 5 W's and 1 H.

The body provides detailed information, quotes, and background to support the lead.

The conclusion summarizes the article and provides a closing thought or call to action.

A quote or quotation is a direct statement from a source, such as an eyewitness, expert, or official, used to provide credibility and a personal perspective. It adds authenticity to the story, making it more engaging and trustworthy for the reader.
