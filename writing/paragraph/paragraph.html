Instructions

  1. Start with a clear topic sentence that states the main idea.
  2. Include supporting details that explain or prove your point.
  3. Use transition words to connect your ideas smoothly.
  4. End with a concluding sentence that summarizes or reinforces the main idea.
  5. Ensure all sentences relate directly to the topic.
Teacher's Tip: A good paragraph is like a mini-essay - it should have a clear beginning, middle, and end!

A paragraph is a group of sentences that develop one main idea. The structure typically includes:

Think of your paragraph as a hamburger - the topic and concluding sentences are the buns, and the supporting details are the juicy fillings!

Topic Sentence: This is the most important sentence that tells the reader what the paragraph is about.

Supporting Sentences: These provide evidence, examples, or explanations that develop the main idea.

Concluding Sentence: This summarizes the main point or provides a transition to the next paragraph.
